Commission on Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice

The Commission on Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice (CCPCJ) is the principal policymaking body of the UN in the field of criminal justice. The CCPCJ aims at improving international action to combat national and transnational crime and the efficiency and fairness of criminal justice administration systems. The CCPCJ offers Member States a forum for exchanging expertise, experience and information in order to develop national and international strategies, and to identify priorities for combating crime. With greater debate on the efficacy of penal punishments and recidivism internationally, the members of the CCPCJ are responsible for creating a model for criminal justice in the volatile times we live in.


Chairing CCPCJ this year is Sarthak Maggon, a final year student at the Symbiosis School of Law, Pune. An avid debater and MUNer, he has been a member of Executive Boards across the country, most notably at the Jindal Global University Model United Nations 2015 and the Symbiosis Law School, Noida MUN 2014.



Chair: Sarthak Maggon

Agenda: International Cooperation in Criminal Matters.
Questions have been raised internationally about the efficacy and need for penal sanctions, and whether we must adopt uniform practices across the world to deal with those who break the law. We meet in the CCPCJ this year to formulate such a code, and find a way to deal with crime in the 21st Century.
